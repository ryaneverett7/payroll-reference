Inbound · Partner → EN

Inbound: The Partner Sends EN a Webhook

When something changes in the payroll system — a new hire, an address update, a compensation change — the partner doesn't wait for EN to ask. They push a notification to EN immediately. This is called a webhook.

Direction

Partner → Employee Navigator

Partner fires a thin webhook to EN's notification endpoint. EN receives it, looks up the employee, and calls Get Employee to fetch the full updated record.

🖨
Inbound
What EN does with it

EN Fetches, Evaluates & Updates

EN calls Get Employee, checks for PII conflicts, evaluates identifier logic, and updates EN's records to stay in sync.

📟
Think of it like this

A webhook is like a pager notification. It doesn't contain the full message — it just says "hey, something changed, go look it up." When a payroll partner sends EN a webhook, it typically only includes a Company ID and an Employee ID. That's it. This intentionally minimal payload is called a "thin event."

EN then uses those two identifiers to call Get Employee on the partner — "give me the full, current record for this person." This two-step design keeps webhooks fast and lightweight while ensuring EN always receives fresh, complete data.

Step-by-Step: What Happens When EN Receives a Webhook

Here's the full sequence from the moment a partner fires a notification to the moment EN's records are updated.

🏢
Partner System · Trigger
A change occurs in payroll
An HR admin updates an employee's address in Paylocity. A new hire is entered in Paychex. A compensation change is made in ADP. The partner's system detects this and fires a notification to EN automatically — no one at EN needs to request it.
🖨
Partner → EN · Thin Webhook
Partner fires a thin notification to EN
The webhook payload is minimal — it contains the CompanyIdentifier (which maps to an EN Client/Subscriber Group) and an EmployeeIdentifier (the unique ID linking this person across both systems). No PII, no field-level details — just "something changed for this person at this company."
🔍
EN · Lookup
EN resolves the Company & Employee identifiers
EN looks up the CompanyIdentifier to find the matching EN client and Subscriber Group. It then looks up the EmployeeIdentifier to find the matching EN employee record. If EN can't find a match, the notification is flagged for analyst review.
📥
EN → Partner · Get Employee
EN calls Get Employee to fetch the full record
Now that EN knows who changed, it calls Get Employee on the partner. The partner returns the complete current record — name, address, DOB, sex, phone, email, compensation, hire/term dates, and more.
🛡
EN · Validation
EN evaluates the incoming data for conflicts
Before writing anything, EN checks for issues:
  • PII conflicts — if the incoming Name, SSN, or DOB doesn't match what's in EN, the change is blocked and flagged for analyst review. EN will not silently overwrite PII.
  • Identifier logic — EN evaluates Active vs. Inactive identifiers. If a termination date is present on a non-primary inactive identifier, it's flagged rather than auto-applied.
  • Blank/invalid SSN — EN will not create or update a record with a blank SSN or certain 1099 tax form types.
✅
EN · Write
EN updates the employee record
If no conflicts are found, EN writes the changes — demographic fields are updated, compensation is recorded, hire/term status is reflected. The sync typically completes within 45–60 seconds of the original change in the partner system.
Important: What Payroll Owns vs. What EN Owns

Not all changes flow both ways. The payroll system is the system of record for certain fields — meaning even if someone edits them in EN, payroll's version wins on the next sync.

  • Payroll → EN only (1-way): New hires, terminations, base compensation (salary/hourly rate), pay effective dates
  • Bi-directional (either system): Name, address, DOB, sex, phone, email
  • EN → Payroll only (1-way): Benefit deductions, employer contributions, enrollment effective dates
Fat Events vs. Thin Events

Some partners (like UKG) explored sending "fat events" — webhooks that include all changed field values directly, so EN doesn't need to make a follow-up Get Employee call. EN's preferred pattern is the thin event + Get Employee because it guarantees EN always has the complete, current record rather than relying on a partial snapshot baked into the webhook payload.

Knowledge Check
Inbound Webhooks
1. An HR admin updates an employee's home address in Paylocity at 2:00 PM. Assuming the integration is live, when should EN reflect this change?
Correct! The thin webhook + Get Employee pattern is real-time. Paylocity fires a notification the moment the address changes, EN calls Get Employee to retrieve the full record, and the update is written to EN within about a minute. No human action is needed unless EN detects a conflict.
2. EN receives a webhook for an employee with the same identifier they already have in place. The Get Employee response shows a different SSN than what EN has on file. What happens?
Correct! When the incoming webhook carries an identifier that already exists in EN, the systems have already established a trusted link for that employee. In this case EN accepts the SSN from payroll and updates it automatically — payroll is the system of record, and the matched identifier confirms this is the same person.
3. Why does EN use the "thin webhook + Get Employee" pattern rather than having the partner embed all changed fields in the webhook itself?
Correct! A fat webhook only captures fields changed at that instant. By following up with Get Employee, EN pulls the full, authoritative current record — catching any other updates that may have happened simultaneously and ensuring nothing is missed.

Under the Hood · EN's Outbound Queue

How EN Queues Up Outbound Transmissions

Before EN can send deduction or demographic data to a partner, it has to decide what needs to be sent and to whom. This is handled by EN's internal notification engine — a system that flags changes and queues them for delivery.

📬
Think of it like this

Imagine a mailroom with a pickup bin. When a deduction changes, EN doesn't immediately call the partner — it first places a slip in the bin saying "this employee's deduction needs to be sent." A background job (the "mail carrier") checks the bin, picks up everything flagged, and delivers it.

The bin is the partner_deduction_notification table. The "ready to deliver" flag is a null processed_date. The push job finds all rows where processed_date IS NULL, sends them, and stamps them with a timestamp to mark them delivered.

Why Only 2 Rows Per Subscriber in partner_api_notification?

Each subscriber only ever has two endpoint types: one for Demographics and one for Deductions. The table doesn't grow with each change — it's a simple on/off switch. When a change happens, EN flips the flag to "needs push." The background push job checks it, pulls all pending notifications for that subscriber, and sends them. Think of it as a light switch, not a receipt log.

How a Deduction Gets Into the Queue

🔄
EN Internal · Change Detected
EN evaluates deduction pairs and flags eligible ones
During processing (enrollment, rate change, OE, QLE, etc.), EN checks matched deduction pairs and marks eligible ones as ForTransmission. The ID from payroll_recurring_deductions is collected for each.
📝
EN Internal · NotificationCriteria
A NotificationCriteria object is created
The eligible deduction ID is wrapped in a NotificationCriteria object and passed to DeductionNotifier.CreateNotifications, which orchestrates everything that follows.
🔎
EN Internal · Subscriber Lookup
EN finds the right subscriber to send to
The NotifiableSubscriberFetcher class queries dbo.view_active_product_subscriptions using the broker/client combination and feature type (Demographics or Deductions). If no specific subscriber is set on the criteria, EN falls back to payday processing logic to identify the correct subscriber group.
💾
EN Internal · WriteNotifications
The notification row is written to the database
DeductionNotificationWriter.WriteNotifications() either inserts a new row if one doesn't exist, or resets processed_date to NULL on the existing row — signaling "this needs to be sent again." It cross-references view_employee_primary_payroll to confirm the subscriber match.
🚀
EN Internal · Push Job
PartnerApiNotifier flips the send flag and delivery begins
PartnerApiNotifier.NotifyViaEndpointTypeId updates partner_api_notification — flipping the boolean for that subscriber/endpoint type to "push now." The push job checks this flag, retrieves all pending rows with a null processed_date, and transmits them as Payroll Deduction or Payroll Employer Contribution calls to the partner.
What "VATI" Means

You may hear the term VATI in engineering discussions. It refers to a row in partner_deduction_notification — one VATI per deduction per subscriber. Each VAT (the partner connection) will only ever have demographic VATIs or deduction VATIs, never both in the same row. The partner_api_notification table is the VAT initiator — the switch that tells the push job to go.

Knowledge Check
EN's Notification Engine
1. A deduction is flagged for transmission. What does a null processed_date in partner_deduction_notification mean?
Correct! A null processed_date is the "pickup slip in the bin" — it means the deduction is queued and waiting for the push job to collect and transmit it. Once sent, the push job stamps the row with the current timestamp. If a change happens again later, the date resets to null to re-queue it.
2. Why does the partner_api_notification table only have 2 rows per subscriber, and why does EN overwrite them instead of adding new rows?
Correct! The partner_api_notification table is a simple switch — not a queue or history log. Two rows: one for Demographics, one for Deductions. When EN needs to push, it flips the flag. The push job checks it, sends everything pending from partner_deduction_notification, and the flag resets. Actual history lives in the partner_deduction_notification rows with their processed_date timestamps.

Outbound: Demographics

Demographics flow both ways — the partner can push changes to EN via webhook (Section 1), and EN can push changes back to the partner when something is updated in EN. Here's how the EN → Partner direction works.

New Hires — Payroll First

For most payroll partners, new hires must be entered in payroll first. The partner fires a webhook to EN, EN calls Get Employee to retrieve the full profile, and EN creates the employee record. The broker or HR admin is alerted on the EN Homepage under "Complete Employees Missing HR Required Fields." If a new hire is created in EN first, the integration may not link correctly until a Payroll ID is manually assigned to the employee in EN.

Heads Up: Paylocity Auto-Notifies Employees

Paylocity is unique — when EN calls Update Employee to push a demographic change, Paylocity automatically sends the employee an email notifying them their profile was updated. This is expected behavior, but worth flagging when setting up clients so HR admins know why employees may receive these notifications.

Knowledge Check
Outbound Demographics
1. An HR admin updates an employee's email address in EN on a Wednesday morning. What does EN do next?
Correct! Demographic outbound pushes are real-time and change-based. EN calls Update Employee on the partner with only the fields that changed — not the full record. This happens automatically within about a minute of the edit being saved in EN.
2. A new employee was added in EN before being set up in payroll. Their deductions aren't transmitting. What's the most likely cause?
Correct! For most partners, new hires need to be added in payroll first so the partner fires a webhook, EN calls Get Employee, and the Payroll ID gets linked to the EN record automatically. When a new hire is created in EN first, no Payroll ID exists — EN has no identifier to include in Update Employee or Payroll Deduction calls. Fix: assign the correct Payroll ID in EN to match the partner's record, then re-push.

Outbound · EN → Partner

Outbound: Deductions & Employer Contributions

This is EN's primary value in the integration: pushing benefit deductions and employer contributions to payroll so they're automatically reflected in employee paychecks. This flows one way only — EN to the partner.

EN calls

Payroll Deduction & Payroll Employer Contribution

Per-pay amounts, deduction codes, effective start dates, and end dates — all driven by benefit enrollments in EN.

📤
One-Way Outbound
Partner receives

Payroll Updates the Employee's Record

The partner applies the deduction to the employee's payroll profile. It appears on the next pay date after the effective start date.

💸
Employee Deductions  Payroll Deduction
The per-paycheck amount withheld from the employee's paycheck for their benefit coverage

EN sends the flat per-pay dollar amount for each benefit the employee is enrolled in — medical, dental, vision, FSA, HSA (employee portion), life, disability, and more. EN does not send percentages or retirement/401(k) contributions.

Each Payroll Deduction call is tied to a deduction code that the payroll partner owns and maps to a specific plan. A single character mismatch between EN's mapped code and the partner's active code will cause a Needs Action failure.

Initial enrollment Open Enrollment QLE / life event Rate recalculation Termination (end-date sent) Deduction code change Enrollment editor adjustment
🏢
Employer Contributions  Payroll Employer Contribution
The per-paycheck amount the employer contributes toward the employee's benefit coverage

Where supported, EN also sends the employer's per-pay contribution via a separate Payroll Employer Contribution call — the portion the company pays toward an employee's medical premium, for example. These use separate employer contribution codes, distinct from the employee deduction code.

Exceptions: Employer contributions for Group Life, Group AD&D, and HSA are not transmitted — but the employee deductions for those plans still are. This is a known limitation across all current partners.

Deduction Codes — The Critical Link

A deduction code is the shared language between EN and the payroll partner. The partner creates and owns these codes in their system (e.g., "MED-EE" for employee medical). EN maps each benefit plan to the correct code during integration setup. If the code in EN doesn't exactly match the active code in payroll, the Payroll Deduction call will fail and appear as a Needs Action error on the Integration Health Dashboard.

Effective Dates vs. Pay Dates

EN sends the benefit effective date as the deduction start date in the Payroll Deduction call — not the pay date. If a deduction start date is November 1st and the next pay date is November 5th, the deduction appears on the November 5th check. EN does not pro-rate for mid-period starts. If the deduction needs to align with a specific pay date, it must be manually adjusted once in the payroll system after EN transmits it.

Knowledge Check
Outbound Deductions & ER Contributions
1. An employee completes a QLE enrollment on a Tuesday afternoon. When does the Payroll Deduction call go to the partner?
Correct! QLE enrollments trigger a real-time Payroll Deduction call. As soon as enrollment is submitted, EN flags the row in partner_deduction_notification, flips the push flag in partner_api_notification, and the background job transmits immediately — no analyst action required.
2. An employer contributes $150/month toward employee HSA accounts. Will EN transmit this via a Payroll Employer Contribution call automatically?
Correct! Employer contributions for Group Life, Group AD&D, and HSA are known exceptions — EN will not send a Payroll Employer Contribution call for these. The employee's own HSA deduction will still transmit via Payroll Deduction. Always flag this expectation when setting up HSA plans with a client.
3. After OE closes, 3 employees show Needs Action because their deduction codes were wrong in EN. The codes are now corrected. What must happen next?
Correct! Once the deduction code is corrected in EN, the system automatically detects the fix and re-fires the Payroll Deduction call within 60 seconds — no manual re-push needed. The corrected rows in partner_deduction_notification are re-queued automatically and the transmission goes out on its own.

Summary · The Full Sync Lifecycle

Putting It All Together

Here's how every piece works together across the full lifecycle of an active payroll integration — from initial setup through everyday real-time sync.

The Complete Two-Way Flow

🔫
One-Time Setup
Connection established, audits completed
Broker/HR connects EN to the partner. Partner maps the Client Identifier and enables webhooks. EN analyst calls Get Employee for all employees during the Demographic Audit to reconcile initial state, then verifies mapped codes and fires Payroll Deduction calls during the Deduction Audit before going live.
🖨
Ongoing · Inbound
Partner → EN: Thin webhook + Get Employee
Any change in the partner system (new hire, address, compensation, termination) → partner fires thin webhook → EN calls Get Employee → EN evaluates for PII conflicts and identifier issues → EN updates the employee record. Completes within ~45–60 seconds.
📤
Ongoing · Outbound Demographics
EN → Partner: Update Employee on any demographic change
Any demographic field change saved in EN → EN calls Update Employee on the partner with only the changed fields. Bi-directional for name, address, DOB, sex, phone, email. Real-time, ~45–60 seconds.
💸
Ongoing · Outbound Deductions
EN → Partner: Payroll Deduction & Payroll Employer Contribution on benefit events
Any enrollment, QLE, rate change, or termination in EN → EN queues the row in partner_deduction_notification → push job fires Payroll Deduction and/or Payroll Employer Contribution with the deduction code, per-pay amount, and start/end date.
📅
Annual · Open Enrollment
Bulk Payroll Deduction push on the Send Deductions Date
On the broker's elected date (early AM EST), EN transmits a comprehensive batch: new Payroll Deduction calls for new plans, updated amounts for renewing employees, end-dated calls for dropped coverage, and old code end + new code start when codes changed at OE.
📊
Ongoing · Monitoring
Integration Health Dashboard tracks every call
In Sync = transmitted successfully. Needs Action = a Get Employee, Update Employee, Payroll Deduction, or Payroll Employer Contribution call failed — requires analyst review (bad code, PII conflict, partner API issue). We're On It = EN detected a transient error and is automatically retrying.

Knowledge Check
The Full Picture
1. A broker says an employee's address was updated in EN three days ago but still doesn't match in payroll. What's your first step?
Correct! Update Employee calls are real-time — if the address hasn't synced after three days, something blocked it. The Integration Health Dashboard is always your first stop. Look for a Needs Action on Demographics for that employee. Common causes: a PII conflict that prevented the write, a partner API outage, or only the deduction integration being live (not demographics yet).
2. Which of the following correctly describes the full flow when a new hire is added in Paychex?
Correct! The full flow: Paychex fires a thin webhook → EN calls Get Employee → EN creates the employee → HR admin is alerted on the EN Homepage under "Complete Employees Missing HR Required Fields." Fully automated, no polling.
3. During Open Enrollment, which types of Payroll Deduction calls does EN send on the Send Deductions Date?
Correct! The OE bulk push is comprehensive — EN sends Payroll Deduction calls covering all four scenarios: brand new enrollments, renewed enrollments with updated amounts, dropped coverage (end-dated), and plans where the deduction code itself changed (old code end-dated, new code started). This is the highest-volume transmission event of the year.

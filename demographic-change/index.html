Step 1: Check the Integration Health Dashboard
Your first move is to open the Payroll Dashboard and go into the Integration Health section. Click into the "In Sync" bucket and search for the employee — you're looking for an "Incoming Demographics" row to see if and when a change notification was received from the partner.
💡
Why the "In Sync" bucket? Even successfully processed incoming changes appear here under "In Sync" — so you can confirm the partner did send EN a notification and that it was pulled in. If the partner sent a change that later failed, it would appear in "Needs Action" or "We're On It" instead.

? The customer fixed the salary in ADP but doesn't want to wait for the next sync. What do they do?

✅ Correct! After fixing the underlying issue in ADP, the customer can immediately trigger a re-sync using the tri-dot Actions icon → Pull from Payroll. EN will pull the updated record and the employee should move to "In Sync."
❌ The customer doesn't have to wait — the Integration Health dashboard lets them manually retry via the Actions icon. This is one of the key self-service features of the dashboard.
❌ No Support call needed. The whole point of the Instructions column and the Actions menu is to let customers self-resolve these issues on their own.

❌ There's no incoming transmission to retry — that's the whole problem. A retry only applies when a failed transmission exists. Since there's nothing in the logs, we need to investigate why no notification came in.
❌ Not yet — we haven't exhausted our troubleshooting steps. Escalation is only appropriate if: (1) no blocked ID, AND (2) the group was already live when the change was made, AND (3) there's still no incoming transmission. That would point to a partner-side issue.

Scenarios & Resolutions
📋 Change was made BEFORE Demographics went Live (Audit Mode)
Option 1 — Employee doesn't exist in EN yet:
Use the Force a demographic change macro to create the record in EN.

Option 2 — Employee exists in EN:
Have the customer go to Integration Health → find the employee's "Incoming Demographics" row → tri-dot icon → Pull from Payroll to manually fetch the change.
🌐 Group was LIVE and change was made AFTER go-live date, but still no transmission
This points to a partner-side issue — ADP/partner did not send EN a change notification.

Resolution: Escalate to Tier 2 for further investigation. The integration was live, the ID is not blocked, and yet no notification was received — this requires deeper partner troubleshooting.
🖱️
How to "Pull from Payroll" in Integration Health:
Integration Health → Find the employee's Incoming Demographics row → click the tri-dot (⋮) Actions icon → select "Pull from Payroll". EN will go fetch the current data from ADP and update the employee's record.
Step 6: Knowledge Check
Answer all three questions to proceed to the summary. These test the key decision points in this troubleshooting flow.

1 A customer says a payroll change isn't updating in EN. What's your FIRST action?

✅ Correct! Always start at the Transmissions log. It tells you whether EN received a notification from the partner — which is the critical fork in the troubleshooting tree.
❌ Re-entering the change may not help if the issue is on EN's side. Start by checking whether EN received a notification first.
❌ Tier 2 escalation is the last resort after all other checks fail. Always investigate first.
❌ Checking blocked IDs comes only if you find no incoming transmission. Start with the transmission log to determine which path to follow.

2 You find a failed Incoming Demographics in Transmissions. On the Integration Health dashboard, it appears in "We're On It." What does this mean?

✅ Correct! "We're On It" = partner API issue (timeout/unavailable). EN handles this automatically overnight. The customer can also manually trigger a Pull from Payroll if they can't wait.
❌ That describes "Needs Action" — where something in EN or the employee's data needs to be fixed. "We're On It" is specifically a partner API availability issue.
❌ A blocked Payroll ID would result in no transmission at all. If there's a failed incoming transmission in the logs, the ID is not blocked.

3 No incoming transmission exists, the employee is not blocked, and the group went live 2 years ago. The change was made yesterday. What do you do?

✅ Correct! All internal causes have been ruled out: no blocked ID, group is live, change was made while live — yet EN never received a notification. This points squarely to a partner-side issue and warrants a Tier 2 escalation.
❌ The "Force a demographic change" macro is for when the employee doesn't exist in EN yet AND the group was still in audit mode. Neither applies here.
❌ "Pull from Payroll" requires an existing transmission row to act on. With no transmission at all, there's nothing to pull from within EN's Integration Health dashboard.
❌ Since the group is live and the ID is not blocked, waiting won't help — EN won't receive the change unless the partner sends a notification. This needs escalation.
